# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The system is used by maintenance admins, department managers, technicians, warehouse or logistics workers, and cleaning staff in an operational CMMS environment. They work in Hebrew, often under time pressure, and need to understand what is urgent, who owns the next action, and whether work is waiting on a person, part, supplier, approval, or closure.

## Product Purpose

Facility Maintenance System is a demo/staging CMMS for managing building maintenance, transport equipment issues, PPE/clothing requests, cleaning control, suppliers, analytics, and user access. Success means the owner can inspect realistic workflows, catch business-rule gaps, and gradually stabilize the app toward production quality without losing the current working demo.

## Brand Personality

Operational, calm, accountable.

The interface should feel like a serious dispatcher tool: dense enough for repeated daily use, clear enough that a manager can immediately see the next required action, and restrained enough that alerts do not all compete at the same volume.

## Anti-references

- Marketing-style landing pages, oversized hero sections, and decorative dashboard cards.
- Noisy ticket cards where status, SLA, risk, owner, waiting reason, and time all appear with equal weight.
- Duplicate settings or duplicated labels that make the user wonder which control is authoritative.
- Decorative icons that imply the wrong domain, such as a hard-hat icon for employee clothing.
- Any new role or checkbox that bypasses the documented unified permissions direction.

## Design Principles

- Show ownership first: every operational item should make the next responsible party clear.
- Reduce repeated signals: do not show the same status twice unless one instance adds new business meaning.
- Keep density useful: compact views are allowed, but labels must have a clear hierarchy.
- Preserve the demo while stabilizing: prefer small reversible changes over broad redesign.
- Use permissions as the access model: roles set defaults, module permissions handle exceptions.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for contrast, focus visibility, readable touch targets, and keyboard-usable controls. Hebrew RTL layout is the default. Motion should be minimal and should not be required to understand state changes.
